COVID-19 Hospital Occupancy Dashboard

With the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, many hospitals across the US are at capacity or are quickly approaching their capacity. Hospitals are managing to cope with the surge in patients through a variety of methods, including opening up new beds, but these are often costly and hurt the level of care that patients receive. However, the burden on hospitals can be reduced if we optimally transfer patients from over-burdened hospitals to nearby hospitals that have available space.

This interactive dashboard finds the best way to transfer COVID-19 patients between hospitals using mathematical optimization models developed by the Johns Hopkins Center for Systems Science and Engineering (JHU CSSE), data on hospitalizations from the US Department of Health and Human Services (HHS), and COVID case forecasts from the US Center for Disease Control (CDC). Visit the home page for more details on our methodology and data. You can use this dashboard to see which hospitals are over capacity, the optimal transfer strategy between them, and when each hospital (and US state) will need additional beds.
