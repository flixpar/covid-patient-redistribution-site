COVID-19 Hospital Capacity Management

With the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, many hospitals across the US are at capacity or are quickly approaching it. Hospitals are coping with the COVID surge through a variety of methods, including opening up new beds, but these are often costly and can hurt the level of care that patients receive. This burden on hospitals can be reduced if we optimally transfer patients from over-capacity hospitals to nearby hospitals that have available space.

To find the optimal number of patients to transfer and where to transfer them to, we use mathematical optimization models developed by the Johns Hopkins Center for Systems Science and Engineering (JHU CSSE), hospitalization data from the US Department of Health and Human Services (HHS), and COVID forecasts from the US Centers for Disease Control (CDC). To learn more, visit the About page.

Dashboard

Our main dashboard page, which investigates the COVID occupancy of hospitals for any state in the US and how transfers could be used between those hospitals. It can be used to analyze:

  • The current and future occupancy of hospitals in the state of your choice
  • If more beds are needed in the state and where
  • The best strategy for transferring patients between hospitals
Nearby Hospitals

A map to find hospitals near you (or any place of interest) and determine which hospitals currently are the least stressed. It includes:

  • The status of hospitals around you
  • A scoring system to advise on which hospitals to go to
Customize Results

For interested users, our Customize Results page can run our models with your choice of parameters, including:

  • Region type (hospital system, Hospital Service Area, Hospital Referral Region, and state)
  • Start and end date
  • Patient transfer budget
